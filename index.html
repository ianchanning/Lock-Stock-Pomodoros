Reminder to self:

1. “Choose a task you’d like to get done”

Translation:

“Pick the thing you’ve been avoiding since the Jurassic period. Doesn’t matter if it’s curing cancer or finally Googling ‘how to adult’—just pick something before your existential dread picks for you. Pro tip: If your to-do list were a person, it’d be haunting your nightmares by now.”

2. “Set the pomodoro for 25 minutes”

Translation:

“Set a timer for 25 minutes. Yes, that timer. The one you’ll stare at like a microwave countdown, bargaining with the universe for a meteor strike to save you from replying to emails. This isn’t a ‘small oath’—it’s a hostage negotiation with your own attention span. Spoiler: You’re both the kidnapper and the negotiator.”

3. “Work on the task until the pomodoro rings”

Translation:

“Hyperfocus for 25 minutes. Or, more accurately: Spend 15 minutes working, 7 minutes wondering if you’re allergic to productivity, and 3 minutes writing down ‘urgent’ distractions like ‘Why do I own 3 staplers?’ or ‘Is my plant judging me?’ Pro move: Burn the distraction list afterward. Fire purifies all sins, including your impulse to reorganize the fridge mid-task.”

Final Note:

The Pomodoro Technique isn’t a productivity hack—it’s a bare-knuckle brawl with your own mediocrity. You’ll lose rounds. You’ll cheat. You’ll reset the timer 8 times to finish this sentence. But hey—at least you’re not scrolling LinkedIn pretending “networking” isn’t corporate voyeurism.
